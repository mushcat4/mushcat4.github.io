Hello!

I am Ian Caldwell, a senior in Computer Science at the University of Texas at San Antonio. I received the Gilman Scholarship to study abroad in Japan in Summer 2023.

Why I Studied In Japan

I began teaching myself Japanese in 2021. I loved it so much I began taking Japanese courses and discovered UTSA's Japan Study Abroad Program.
Despite the study abroad not fitting into my degree plan, it was an amazing opportunity to immerse myself in a foreign country's distinct history and traditions.
Studying abroad allowed me to decide whether I wanted to return to Japan. As soon as I got home, I started looking for semester exchange programs for Spring 2024.

What I Did in Japan

The program was a blend of traditional Japanese culture and modern life. The ancient capital of Kyoto was home to many shrines and temples surrounded by beautiful nature. On the other hand, densely populated cities like Osaka showed the vibrant nightlife of Japan.
Some other day trips were Himeji Castle, Nara Park, Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, Wazuka Tea Farm, and many more! Additionally, we attended classes and had the opportunity to speak with the students and professors at UTSAâ€™s sister university in Kyoto.

How I Paid For Studying Abroad:
The Gilman Scholarship

Even though I had never left Texas before studying abroad, I knew that it would cost a lot. Receiving The Gilman Scholarship paid for the majority of the expenses and made my experience in Japan possible. As a gateway for studying abroad, it offers underrepresented individuals receiving the Pell Grant an opportunity of a lifetime.
